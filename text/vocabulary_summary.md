# Summary of IELTS Academic Writing Task 1 Vocabulary

This document summarizes the vocabulary and phrases found in `task 1 vocabulary.txt`, categorized by their use in describing data, charts, and graphs.

## 1. Degrees of Change
*   **Small Changes:** modest, slight, minor, tiny, insignificant, minute, subtle, marginal.
*   **Big Changes:** significant, dramatic, substantial, huge, massive, enormous, major, sharp, overwhelming, considerable.

## 2. Describing Trends
*   **Positive Trends:** rise, increase, improve, rocket, soar, jump, ascend, boost, peak, uplift, climb, upsurge, double/triple.
*   **Negative Trends:** fall, decrease, decline, plummet, plunge, drop, descend, slump, reduce, dip, halve.
*   **Neutral/Stable Trends:** maintain, remain, stay, plateau, even out, stabilize, unchanged, stable, steady, static, consistent.
*   **Fluctuation:** fluctuate, oscillate, show a palpitation, ups and downs.

## 3. Structural Phrases
*   **Introductions:** "The diagram shows...", "The given pie charts represent...", "The chart gives information about...", "The bar graph compares...".
*   **General Trends:** "Overall...", "In general...", "Generally speaking...", "It is obvious...", "A glance at the graph reveals that...".
*   **Detailing Data:** "Turning to the details...", "The diagram reveals that...", "It is interesting to note that...", "It is crystal clear/lucid that...".

## 4. Data and Time Vocabulary
*   **Time References:** "From 1990 to 2000", "By 1995", "In the first half of the year", "Within a time span of ten years", "Previous year".
*   **Percentages & Fractions:** "10% increase", "exactly a quarter (25%)", "nearly one-third (32%)", "around a half (49%)", "well over three quarters (79%)".
*   **Proportions:** "A tiny portion", "An insignificant minority", "A significant majority", "A large proportion".

## 5. Approximation Phrases
Used for non-exact data points:
*   Approximately, nearly, roughly, almost, about, around, more or less, just over, just under, a little more than.
